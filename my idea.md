Product Name

FacilityOS

Tagline

Give every piece of equipment a memory.

Product Overview

FacilityOS is a premium equipment care and facility operations platform designed for gyms, sports facilities, performance centres, studios and other high-use physical environments.

The app helps teams keep equipment, rooms and facility areas operating at the highest possible standard by combining preventative care tasks, QR-code fault reporting, live equipment status, manager spot checks, replacement intelligence and AI-powered operational insights.

At its simplest, FacilityOS answers four key questions:

What needs doing?
What has been done?
What is broken?
What is starting to become a bigger problem?

The app is designed to be painfully simple for users and staff, but intelligent in the background. Public users can scan a QR code and report an issue in seconds. Staff can see exactly what cleaning, maintenance or inspection tasks need completing. Managers can see live operational standards, identify equipment at risk, check work quality and understand patterns across the facility.

FacilityOS is not just a fault-reporting tool. It is a digital care layer for facilities.

Core Concept

Every room, area and piece of equipment has its own digital profile.

This profile contains:

live traffic light status
equipment image
location
cleaning history
maintenance history
inspection history
fault history
out-of-order history
care tasks
manager spot-check history
replacement review status
AI-generated operational insights

The QR code attached to each item acts as the bridge between the physical facility and the digital system.

When someone scans the QR code, they see a clean, premium status page for that specific item. For example, if they scan a Technogym treadmill, they see the treadmill image, its current status, when it was last cleaned, when it was last maintained, whether any faults are active, and a simple option to report an issue.

This makes the system feel specific, trustworthy and connected to the real facility.

How the App Functions

FacilityOS works around a simple operational loop:

1. Equipment Gets a Care Plan

Each piece of equipment or facility area has a care plan.

This care plan defines the cleaning, maintenance and inspection work required to keep it functioning properly.

For example, a treadmill may need:

daily touchpoint cleaning
weekly emergency stop check
weekly belt inspection
monthly belt alignment review
annual service review

A cable machine may need:

daily pad and handle cleaning
weekly attachment inspection
weekly cable movement check
monthly guide rod cleaning and greasing
periodic cable condition inspection

This makes the product proactive rather than purely reactive.

2. Staff Complete Care Tasks

Staff use the app to see what care tasks are due.

Tasks are grouped clearly by area, equipment type or priority so the team can work efficiently.

For example:

Cable Area Care Round
6 machines · Daily cleaning · Due before 10:00

Treadmill Weekly Checks
10 machines · Emergency stops and belt condition · Due today

Leg Press 02
Grease guide rods · QR confirmation required

When a task is completed, the system records the completion time, who completed it, and any required evidence.

This creates a reliable history of care.

3. Public Users Can Report Faults

If a user notices something wrong, they scan the QR code and submit a fault report.

They can choose a category, describe the problem and upload a photo.

The report is automatically linked to the correct equipment, room or facility area.

This removes the common problem of vague reports such as:

“The treadmill is broken.”

Instead, the manager receives a structured report connected to the exact equipment and its recent history.

4. Staff Can Mark Equipment Out of Order

If a staff member identifies that a piece of equipment is unsafe or not suitable for use, they can mark it as out of order.

This immediately changes the equipment status to red.

The public QR page updates to show that the item is currently unavailable, and the system begins tracking how long the item remains out of service.

This is important because downtime is one of the strongest indicators of reliability and operational impact.

5. Managers Review and Resolve Issues

Managers can view all active issues and prioritise what needs action.

They can assign work, update status, request follow-up, resolve issues and return equipment to service when appropriate.

Each issue is stored in the equipment’s history, creating a permanent record of faults, responses and resolutions.

Over time, this builds a detailed memory of how each item performs.

6. Manager Spot Checks Maintain Standards

FacilityOS includes a manager spot-check system.

Managers do not need to check every completed task. Instead, the system randomly selects a sample of completed work for review.

This works like professional assurance systems, where a sample is checked to confirm standards are being maintained.

If spot checks pass, the sample size can remain small.

If spot checks fail, the system increases the sample size because there may be an operational issue, training gap or standards problem.

This creates a fair and intelligent quality-control loop.

The principle is:

Trust the team, but verify enough work to know standards are being maintained.

7. The App Detects Patterns

As the system collects data, FacilityOS begins to identify patterns.

It can identify:

equipment that keeps failing
tasks that are frequently missed
areas with repeated cleaning issues
faults that return after being resolved
equipment with increasing downtime
maintenance tasks that are overdue
locations where standards are slipping
items that may need replacement review

This turns daily operational activity into useful intelligence.

8. Replacement Intelligence Flags Equipment at Risk

FacilityOS includes replacement intelligence.

This does not need to involve financial data at the beginning.

Instead, the system looks at operational signals such as:

repeated faults
repeat out-of-order events
extended downtime
failed inspections
recurring maintenance issues
fault severity
issues returning after repair
poor spot-check outcomes

If a piece of equipment appears to be becoming unreliable, the system can flag it for replacement review.

For example:

Treadmill 04 has been marked out of order several times in recent months and has repeated belt-related faults after maintenance. Consider reviewing whether this item remains reliable enough for service.

This helps managers justify future investment decisions with evidence.

9. AI Supports the Manager

AI should be used as a practical assistant, not a gimmick.

The AI layer helps by:

summarising equipment history
identifying repeated faults written in different ways
suggesting issue categories
suggesting priority levels
identifying possible safety concerns
suggesting next actions
creating weekly manager summaries
helping generate care plans for new equipment
explaining why equipment may need replacement review

AI should never replace the manager’s decision. It should support judgement by highlighting patterns that may otherwise be missed.

The role of AI in FacilityOS is:

“This looks like a pattern. You may want to review it.”

What Makes FacilityOS Different

FacilityOS is not simply a reporting system.

It combines five layers that are usually disconnected:

1. Equipment Identity

Every item has a profile, QR code and live status.

2. Preventative Care

The team knows what needs doing before problems become faults.

3. Fault Reporting

Users and staff can report issues quickly and accurately.

4. Manager Assurance

Spot checks confirm standards without requiring managers to check everything.

5. Intelligence

The system learns from faults, downtime, maintenance history and failed checks to highlight patterns and replacement risks.

This makes FacilityOS more than a digital checklist.

It becomes a memory and intelligence layer for the facility.
